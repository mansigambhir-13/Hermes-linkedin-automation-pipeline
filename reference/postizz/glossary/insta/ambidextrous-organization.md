## Caption

Most companies are built to do one thing well. A rare few run two clocks at once.

An ambidextrous organization exploits today's business for profit while a structurally separate unit explores tomorrow's, different metrics, different culture, joined only at the top. O'Reilly and Tushman found the pattern studying breakthrough efforts across nine industries.

The hard trick is not choosing between today and tomorrow. It is running both.

Defensible in 15 minutes.
