## Caption

Zappos pays new hires to quit. After four weeks of training, every recruit is offered a few thousand dollars to walk away.

Almost nobody takes it, only two or three in a hundred. The ones who stay have chosen the culture over easy cash, and they engage harder for having chosen. The offer turns a passive hire into a committed one.

The cheapest way to keep people is to give them a clean chance to leave.

Read it. Now defend it.
