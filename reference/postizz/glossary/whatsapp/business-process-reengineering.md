## Caption (copy-paste)

*Business Process Reengineering*

Most improvement asks how to do the same work faster. Reengineering asks a ruder question: why do this work at all?

*Michael Hammer* coined business process reengineering in 1990, not tuning a process but tearing it up and redesigning around the outcome. Done well it collapses cost and time at once. Done as a slogan it just means layoffs.

The biggest gains hide in the steps you stop doing entirely.

Read it. Now defend it.
