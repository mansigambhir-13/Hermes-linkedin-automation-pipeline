## Caption (copy-paste)

*Convention Blindness*

Say a price out loud often enough and it starts to feel like a law of nature. Batteries cost six hundred dollars a kilowatt-hour, everyone knew it.

Except the raw materials cost about eighty. The rest was *convention blindness*: mistaking an industry's habit for physics. And it deepens with experience, the more years you spend inside a field, the more its rules feel like gravity.

The most expensive assumptions are the ones nobody questions anymore.

Read it. Now defend it.
