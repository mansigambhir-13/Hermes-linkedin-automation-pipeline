## POST COPY

> 31 January 2024. After the market closes, the RBI orders Paytm Payments Bank to stop accepting deposits, top-ups, and credits after 29 February.
>
> Vijay Shekhar Sharma has less than a day before the market opens.
>
> Here is what most retellings miss: this was not a bolt from the blue. The RBI had barred the same bank from onboarding new customers in March 2022 — almost two years earlier. The warning had been live the entire time.
>
> Three choices on the table that night.
>
> One: fight it. Frame the order as regulatory overreach, mobilise the founder narrative, rally retail investors.
>
> Two: go silent. Let the lawyers respond, say nothing as founder until the technical picture is clear.
>
> Three: concede and pivot. Accept the RBI's authority in public, express regret, move the business to partner banks before the deadline.
>
> What would you do?
>
> (Read the room first. The audience that matters is not retail investors. It is the regulator that spent two years watching, and the banking partners Paytm would need by the weekend.)
>
> Sharma chose three. He signalled regret and cooperation, and One97 moved fast to route payments through other banks. The stock still hit its 20% lower circuit on 1 February and kept falling — close to a third of One97's market value gone in a week.
>
> What most people miss about this decision: it was never defiance versus surrender. By 2024 the defiant option was already foreclosed. Two years of unheeded warnings had spent it. The only move left was to protect the business that could still be saved. Sharma read that correctly. The market punished the bank he could not.
